History of Anubis

Some of the earliest recorded depictions of Anubis were in Egypt's Early Dynastic period. Depictions of Anubis during this time were of Anubis in full animal form. He was depcited as a jackal, which many believed was connected to the common occurence of wild dogs digging up graves.

During the Middle Kingdom, Anubis was replaced by Osiris as the principal God of the Dead. Additionally, ancient texts disagree on Anubis' ancestry. More recent texts assert that Anubis is the son of Nephthys and Osiris and was later adoped by Isis.

In the Ptolomaic period, while Egypt was under the rule of Greece, Anubis merged with Hermes and was referred to as Hermanubis.
